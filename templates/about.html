About Me

This is what I do.

I have always liked to think of myself as a what I call a modern-day renaissance man. I have a wide range of interests and hobbies that have given me an equally wide range of skills. A few that are top of the list are programming, leather working, laser engraving, model trains, and building things from wood. Even as a kid I was interested in everything! And my father had friends with a wide range of professions. He would often have them come over and help work on things around our house. That is how I learned to do construction, lay concrete, work with electrical wiring, roofing and painting houses, aspects of engineering, and how to maintain an HVAC system all from helping my dad and his friends around the house.

When it came to college, I again found myself with a wide range of interests. That led me to get a degree in the college of arts and sciences, which required me to take art, literature, science, philosophy, and foreign language along with my business major in economics. And it was that requirement that led me to "experiment" with different ideas for a major. At first, I was going to be a physicist. And I did very well in the classes, but I hated it. It was pure rote repetition. Here is a problem, classify it into a known type, and calculate the result based on a set formula. Not exactly riveting work. I am sure it would have become interesting, but I couldn't wait for that.

Luckily, I had signed up for an intro to economics course at the same time I was questioning physics as my major. The idea of using math to explain the behavior of people and how that guides the economy was, to say the least, intriguing. And the fact it is the science of optimization made it that much more interesting. I was hooked almost immediately. No more simple math or using only pre-defined formulas. Now there was logic, calculus, advanced statistics, the works. It just clicked with me like I was born to do it. The head of the econ department even asked to see me one day after I aced his final exam just to tell me how impressed he was and that I would make a wonderful analyst one day.

Turns out he was right as that became my initial career and how I developed advanced skills in Excel, VBA, PBI. And that led me to a career in business intelligence (BI) which takes analytics to the next level. As a side interest I took up python programming which turned out to be right in line with the future of BI as it is now the go-to language for data science, machine learning and AI. So that is where I am today.
